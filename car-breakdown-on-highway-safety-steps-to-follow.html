What to Do When Your Car Breaks Down on a Highway: A Clever, Calm Guide

Nothing details a road trip quite like a sudden car break down on a highway. Stay calm, keep safety first, and follow this smart checklist. You’ll get back on the road faster than you think.

Safety comes first

If your car is moving, pull over to the shoulder or a safe turnout as far away from traffic as possible.

Turn on hazard lights and, if you have them, set up warning triangles or flares. If you have passengers, especially kids or pets, keep them strapped in and away from traffic.

If you must exit the vehicle, use the passenger side and watch for oncoming traffic.

Assess the situation without panicking

Try to determine if the issue is something obvious (gas gauge, obvious breakdown sounds, overheating) or if you’re unsure.

If you’re in immediate danger (smoke, fire, or a noticeable fuel leak), move away and call

Tips to prevent future highway trouble

Regular maintenance: check fluids, tires, brakes, and battery before long trips.

Carry a roadside emergency kit: flashlight, jumper cables, first-aid kit, water, non-perishable snacks, reflective gear, and a compact tool set.

Keep your vehicle’s emergency numbers handy and store a digital version of your important documents.

Don’t attempt risky fixes on the highway

Avoid trying to fix major problems yourself on the roadway.
A brief attempt to check something safe (like a loose hose or battery connections) is okay only if you can do so from a safe position and without opening the hood into oncoming traffic.

When help arrives: understanding car towing

If you need to move the vehicle, a professional car towing service will handle it safely.
Describe the issue clearly to the tow operator so they bring the right equipment and, if needed, the right destination (repair shop, dealer, or your home).
Confirm the destination and the estimated arrival time. If you’re using insurance or membership benefits, have those details ready.

After the tow: next steps for car break down

Get a written estimate for the repair work and confirm payment methods before service begins.
If you’re stranded, arrange alternative transportation and communicate timelines to work, family, or teammates.
Keep receipts for any towing or rental costs for possible reimbursement through insurance or your employer.
